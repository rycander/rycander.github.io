ABOUT ME

I have always loved working with computers from a young age. After giving non-CS subject a fair shot in collge, I decided to follow my dreams, and major in CS.

After graduating, I was happy to land my first job, a position on the graphics hardware software validation team, as I have always been interested in computer graphics. There, I was mostly writing and debugging Ruby, as Intel's test suite consisted of Ruby files that generated assembly language tests.

Over my time there, I helped the team through tight deadlines, and to modify tests for functionality across multiple versions of Ruby, and fixed a large part of the .

Unfortunately, Intel went through some downsizing and I happened to be the highest paid engineer on my team, so I was let go.

However, I consider that to have been a blessing in disguise. It gave me time to think about what I really wanted to do after getting some initial exposure.

I felt really drawn to web because that’s where one developer can have the biggest impact. In my free time, I taught myself web development by downloading various web frameworks and trying them out.

While I feel that I learned a lot during this period, I was not satisfied with how quickly I was learning. I was considering going back to school, when I heard about App Academy.

They had a selective course with only a 5% admission rate, and the most rigorous application process I saw, so I knew I’d be surrounded by people who would challenge me. After having graduated, I am happy to report that my initial impression was correct.
